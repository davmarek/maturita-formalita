1. Holidays and Celebrations in the Czech Republic


Spring

  • Easter - Not fixed date, ending of March - first half of April

    • Willow canes - "korbáče"
    • Whipping - "mrskání"

  • Mother’s Day - First sunday of May

    • Celebration honoring the mother of the family.

  • Labours Day - 8th May

    • We commemorate the end of the 2nd World War in Europe in 1946.

Summer

  • St. Cyril and Methodius Day - 5th July

    • It is an anniversary of the arrival of Cyril and Methodius to Great Moravia in 863.

  • Jan Hus Day - 6th July

    • Celebrated to commemorate a religion reformer Jan Hus who was burned at the stake i Kostnice in 1415.

Autumn

  • St. Wenceslas Day - 28th September

    • Wenceslas, patron of the Czech statem was Premyslid duke of Bohemia, on September 28th 935 was murdered by his younger brother Boleslav I.

  • Independent Czechoslovak State Day - 28th October

    • This day the Czechoslovak state declared independence at the end of the First World War, in 1918.

  • Day of the Fight for Freedom and Democracy - 17th November

    • First in 1939 when we were occupied by Germany as the Protectorate of Bohemia and Moravia, Czech students demonstrated against Nazi regime.
    • Then in 1989, the Velvet Revolution started also as a student demonstration against communist party.

Winter

  • Christmas Eve Day - 24th December

    • Described in the detail section

  • 1st Christmas Holiday - 25th December

  • 2st Christmas Holiday (Christmas Feast and St. Stephen's Day) - 26th December

    • On St. Stephen's Day, children, students, teachers, and the poor used to go around people's homes singing Christmas carols. Nowadays, families stay at home and relax or visit relatives and friends to share the special time.

  • New Year Day, Restoration of Czech Independence Day - 1st January

    • Celebrating the beginning of a new year.
    • We also celebrate the establishment of the independent Czech Republic. It happened in 1993 when Czechoslovakia split into the Czech Republic and Slovakia

  • Valentine’s Day - 14th February

    • 14., on Valentine’s Day, boys and girls send each other anonymous greeting cards called valentines.

Detail - Christmas


Date/Seasons of the year

  • Christmas was originally a christian holiday. It was associated with the birth of Jesus Christ.

Customs/Traditions

  • We celebrate Christmas in winter, exactly from the 24th of December to 26th of December.
  • At the present Christmas is mainly connected with giving presents. The 24th of December some people don’t eat all day and hope to see a gold pig in the evening. We have Christmas dinner and then we go to the christmas tree where are presents.
  • Some families maintain traditions like cutting an apple, dropping boats, lead pouring, kisses under mistletoe or singing christmas carols.
  • At the midnight we can visit midnight mass.

Symbols

  • Symbols of Christmas are for example christmas tree, bethlehem, baby jesus, presents, apples etc.

Meals

  • At Christmas Eve dinner we eat pea soup and carp with potato salad. People also bake christmas cake.
